# Residence Card (Zairyu Card) Complete Guide

## Overview

The Residence Card (zairyu kaado) is the single most important document for any foreign national living in Japan with a mid-to-long-term visa. It serves as your primary ID, proves your legal status, and must be carried at all times by law. Understanding your obligations around this card -- updating it, renewing it, and what to do if it is lost -- is essential to staying in legal compliance and avoiding fines or visa problems.

## Who Needs This

- All foreign nationals residing in Japan with a period of stay of more than 3 months
- This includes holders of: work visas (Engineer/Specialist in Humanities, Intra-company Transferee, Highly Skilled Professional, etc.), student visas, spouse visas, dependent visas, long-term resident visas, and permanent resident visas
- **Who does NOT get one:** Tourists (short-term stay up to 90 days), diplomats, and those with special permanent resident status (they receive a Special Permanent Resident Certificate instead)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Understanding Your Residence Card

**What is on the card:**
1. **Front side:** Your name, date of birth, nationality/region, address, status of residence (visa type), period of stay, expiration date, whether work is permitted, photo, and a card number.
2. **Back side:** Space for address updates (up to 3 changes can be printed), information on permission for activities outside your status, and an IC chip area.
3. **IC chip:** The card contains an IC chip with encrypted data that can be read by immigration officials and is used for certain government services.

**Work permission indicator:**
- The card states whether you are permitted to work. Common notations:
  - "Engagement in the activities listed in the left column (employment) is permitted" -- you can work within the scope of your visa.
  - "Not permitted to engage in employment" -- you cannot work (common on dependent and student visas unless you have extra permission).
  - If you have a "Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted" (shikaku gai katsudou kyoka), this is noted on the back of the card.

### Obligation to Carry Your Residence Card

**The law:** Under the Immigration Control and Refugee Recognition Act, you must carry your Residence Card at all times.

**What this means in practice:**
1. Keep the physical card on your person whenever you leave your home.
2. Police officers and immigration officials can ask to see it at any time.
3. You may also need it for everyday tasks: picking up packages at the post office, checking into hotels, signing contracts, or verifying your identity.

**Penalty for not carrying it:** A fine of up to 200,000 yen. In practice, enforcement is usually a warning for first-time incidents, but repeated non-compliance can lead to formal penalties.

**Tip:** Take a clear photograph of both sides of your card and store it securely on your phone and in cloud storage. This is not a legal substitute for carrying the card, but it can help if you need your card number or details in an emergency and can assist in the replacement process if the card is lost.

### Updating Your Address (Within 14 Days of Moving)

**When required:** Every time you move to a new address within Japan.

**Deadline:** Within 14 days of moving into your new residence.

**Process:**
1. Go to the **city/ward office (shiyakusho or kuyakusho)** of your NEW address.
2. If moving between municipalities, first submit a **Moving-Out Notification (tenshutsu todoke)** at the office of your OLD address, then submit a **Moving-In Notification (tennyuu todoke)** at your new address's office.
3. If moving within the same municipality, submit a **Change of Address Notification (tenkyou todoke)** at the same office.
4. Bring your Residence Card and passport.
5. The clerk will print your new address on the back of the Residence Card.
6. Your juminhyo (resident register) is updated simultaneously.

**Important:** If the back of your card runs out of space for address updates (after 3 changes), immigration will issue a new card with the current address.

**Penalty for late notification:** Fine of up to 200,000 yen.

### Changing Jobs (Notification Required Within 14 Days)

**When required:** When you change employers, or when there is a significant change to your contract (such as a change in your organization's name or your role).

**Deadline:** Within 14 days of the change.

**Process:**
1. Submit a **Notification of the Contracting Organization** to the Immigration Services Agency.
2. You can submit this:
   - **Online:** Through the Immigration Services Agency's electronic notification system at https://www.ens-immi.moj.go.jp/
   - **By mail:** Send the notification form to the Tokyo Regional Immigration Services Bureau (even if you live outside Tokyo for this particular notification).
   - **In person:** At your nearest immigration office.
3. Required information: Your Residence Card number, name, date of birth, nationality, address, previous employer details, new employer details, and dates of separation and new employment.
4. **This notification does NOT change your visa type.** If your new job falls outside the scope of your current visa category, you must apply for a **Change of Status of Residence (zairyuu shikaku henkou)** before starting the new job.

**Before changing jobs, consider applying for a Certificate of Authorized Employment (shuurou shikaku shoumeisho):**
- This document confirms whether your new job is within the scope of your current visa.
- Apply at your local immigration office.
- Processing takes 1-3 months.
- Not mandatory but strongly recommended, especially if your new role is in a different field.

### Renewing Your Residence Card (Visa Extension)

**When required:** Before your current period of stay expires.

**When to apply:** You can apply for renewal starting **3 months before** your expiration date. Do not wait until the last minute.

**Process:**
1. Go to your regional Immigration Services Bureau (nyuukoku kanri kyoku) or its branch office.
2. Fill out the **Application for Extension of Period of Stay (zairyuu kikan kousin kyoka shinseisho)**.
3. **Required documents:**
   - Application form (available at immigration or downloadable from the Immigration Services Agency website)
   - Passport
   - Current Residence Card
   - A 4cm x 3cm photograph (taken within the last 3 months)
   - Documents proving continued employment: employment certificate from your company, tax payment certificates (nouzei shoumeisho), withholding tax slip (gensen choushu hyou)
   - Additional documents depending on your visa type (check the Immigration Services Agency website for your specific category)
4. Pay the processing fee: 4,000 yen (revenue stamp, purchased at the immigration office or post office).
5. Processing time: Typically 2 weeks to 2 months. In busy periods or complex cases it can take longer.
6. You will receive a postcard notification when the decision is made. Return to immigration with your passport, current Residence Card, and the postcard to pick up your new card.

**What if your visa expires while the renewal is being processed?**
- If you applied before the expiration date, you can legally remain in Japan for up to 2 months past the expiration while the application is pending (this is noted on a sticker placed in your passport or on a designated activity permission).
- You must not leave Japan while the application is pending unless you have specific permission.

**What if your application is denied?**
- You will be given a short period (usually 30-31 days with "Designated Activities" status) to prepare to leave Japan, or to appeal/reapply.

### What to Do If You Lose Your Residence Card

**Deadline:** Apply for reissuance within **14 days** of discovering the loss.

**Process:**
1. **File a police report (fun-shitsu todoke).** Go to the nearest police station or koban and report the lost card. Get a copy of the report or the report number.
2. Go to your **regional Immigration Services Bureau**.
3. Fill out the **Application for Reissuance of Residence Card (zairyuu kaado no saikou-fu shinsei)**.
4. **Required documents:**
   - Application form
   - Passport
   - A 4cm x 3cm photograph
   - Document proving the loss (police report copy)
   - Fee: There is no application fee for reissuance due to loss, but if reissuance is due to damage or contamination, there is typically no fee either.
5. Processing time: Usually same day or within a few days for straightforward cases.

**If your card is stolen:** Also file a theft report (higai todoke) with the police in addition to the immigration application.

**If you lose it outside Japan:** Contact the nearest Japanese embassy or consulate for instructions. You will also need to deal with this upon re-entry at the airport.

### What to Do If Your Card Is Damaged

- If the card is cracked, the IC chip is not readable, or the printed information is faded/illegible, apply for reissuance at immigration.
- Bring the damaged card, your passport, a photo, and the application form.

### The Digital Residence Card System

**Recent developments:** The Immigration Services Agency has been modernizing the Residence Card system.

**Key updates:**
1. **IC chip verification:** The IC chip on the Residence Card can be read using NFC-enabled smartphones. The Immigration Services Agency offers a free app called "Residence Card Verification App" that allows employers and others to verify the authenticity of a card.
2. **Online applications:** Many immigration procedures that previously required in-person visits can now be done online through the Immigration Services Agency's online application system. Eligible procedures include extension of period of stay, change of status of residence, and re-entry permits.
3. **MyNumber Card integration:** The Japanese government has been exploring integration between the Residence Card and MyNumber Card systems. As of recent policy, the Residence Card functionality is being merged into the MyNumber Card, allowing a single card for both purposes. Check the Immigration Services Agency website for the latest implementation timeline.
4. **Employer verification system:** Employers can use the Immigration Services Agency's online system to verify a foreign employee's work authorization using the card number and other details.

**What this means for you:** Keep your card details and IC chip in good condition, as digital verification is increasingly common. Ensure your address and employer information are always up to date.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Leaving your Residence Card at home.** Many foreigners keep it in a desk drawer or safe for fear of losing it. The law requires you to carry it at all times. Consider using a card holder in your wallet.

2. **Forgetting to update your address after moving.** This is one of the most common violations. Even moving within the same city requires notification. Set a reminder for yourself.

3. **Not notifying immigration when changing jobs.** Even if you stay in the same visa category, the notification is required. Many people do not realize this is a separate obligation from simply starting a new job.

4. **Starting a new job that falls outside your visa scope without changing your status.** For example, an Engineer visa holder who starts working as a language teacher may be outside their visa scope. Always check before switching.

5. **Waiting too long to renew.** If your visa expires and you have not applied for extension, you become an overstayer. Apply at least 1-2 months before expiration.

6. **Not updating the card after renewing your passport.** If your passport number changes (due to renewal or reissuance), you should update this information at immigration.

7. **Lending your Residence Card to someone.** It is illegal to lend, borrow, or transfer Residence Cards. This applies to both the lender and the user.

8. **Losing the card and not reporting it within 14 days.** Late reporting can cause problems at immigration and affect future applications.

## Key Deadlines

| Action | Deadline | Consequence of Missing |
|--------|----------|----------------------|
| Register/update address after moving | Within 14 days | Fine up to 200,000 yen |
| Notify immigration of employer change | Within 14 days | May affect visa renewal; fine possible |
| Apply for reissuance after loss | Within 14 days of discovery | Complications with legal status |
| Apply for visa extension | Before expiration (start 3 months early) | Overstay status; deportation risk |
| Return Residence Card when leaving Japan permanently | At the airport upon departure | Required by law; hand to immigration officer |
